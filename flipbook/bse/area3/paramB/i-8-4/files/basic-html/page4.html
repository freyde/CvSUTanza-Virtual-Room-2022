               Evaluation of the Case

                       The Philippines is principally a Christian country, particularly a Roman Catholic nation.
               Although  there  is  a  separation  of  the  church  and  the  state,  lawmakers  of  the  Philippines
               consider deriving morals from the Bible in legislating law. The main focus of this case analysis is
               the  issue  of  the  legalization  of  same-sex  marriage  in  our  country.  The  religious  principles,
               culture and social acceptance, and marriage provisions of the Family Code of the Philippines
               were  the main hindrances that dismissed the petition of the LGBTQ+ community. This is the
               reason  why  as  of  now,  the  Philippines  does  not  recognize same-sex marriages between its
               citizens and still has a solid and ongoing debate regarding the legality of marriage for same-sex
               couples.
