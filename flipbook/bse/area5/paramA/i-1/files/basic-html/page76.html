                       Completion Rate measures the percentage of grade 1 who graduate in elementary

               education.  It can be seen that the completion rates of boys in the elementary school are
               slightly higher than those of the girls.
                       Graduation Rate refers to the number of graduates from the last grade of primary

               education, regardless of age, expressed as a percentage of the population at the theoretical
               graduation age for primary.  The percentage of girls who graduate from the elementary level
               is slightly higher than the percentage of boys for the three school years under comparison.

                       Promotion Rate is used to assess the extent of pupils who are promoted to the next
               grade level.  The promotion rates for both boys and girls indicate high internal efficiency of the

               educational system as they near 100%. Again, the promotion rate of girls is slightly higher than
               the promotion rate of boys for the three school years under comparison.
                       Repetition  Rate  determines  the  magnitude  of  pupils  who  repeat  a  grade  level.

               However, there are more girls who repeat the same grade level for the next school year than
               boys.

                       School-Leaver Rate covers both pupils who do not finish a particular grade level as
               well as those who finish but fail to enroll in the next grade level the following school year.  A
               slight difference exists in the percentage of boys leaving the school than girls for the three

               years under comparison.
